Buying a car with personality is a patience and judgement exercise. Whether
you're chasing an MX-5, a Golf MK2 or a 240Z, the principles stay the same.
This guide captures the method we follow at Jinba before recommending a
purchase.

## Paperwork first

Don't start with the car, start with the documents. No history means no
trust, and no trust means no reasonable price.

- Ask for up-to-date inspection reports plus at least two previous ones.
- Check the vehicle registry for suspicious transfers, liens, or debts.
- Confirm that the color and plate match the technical sheet.

### Signs of a badly repaired car

If an owner won't let you pull the technical sheet or photograph the chassis
plates, walk away. An honest seller welcomes inspection.

## Mechanical inspection

Invest in a professional pre-purchase inspection. Paying €150 to a trusted
workshop can save you thousands.

1. Cold compression test across all cylinders.
2. Visible oil leaks: pan, seals, head gasket.
3. Clutch health and clean shifts into every gear.
4. Bodywork: magnet along wheel arches, sills and boot floor.

## Negotiation and closing

Once the car convinces you, negotiate with numbers, not emotions. Arrive
with appraisals in hand and comparable offers. Don't overpay because you
love the first colour you see — there are more cars out there.

1. Use the market median as your anchor.
2. Deduct outstanding consumables (oil, tyres, cambelt).
3. Close with a written contract and a traceable payment method.

## After the purchase

The first thousand kilometres are diagnostic. Do a full service, flush
fluids, and document everything. Your soulful car will thank you with
years of life.
