The Mazda MX-5 NA is one of the most reliable and entertaining roadsters ever
built, but the years keep passing. Only take a good example home if you check
what actually matters. This guide covers the critical points that separate a
healthy NA from a hidden project.

## Before moving the car

Walk around the car before turning the key. Look for poorly repaired impacts,
patchwork paintwork and factory stickers still in place.

- Match the chassis and engine numbers against the paperwork.
- Look inside the soft top: rust stains around the hinges mean chronic water
  ingress.
- Lift the boot carpets — this is corrosion hotspot number one.

### Paint and bodywork

Run a magnet along the rear wheel arches. If it won't stick, there's filler
under the paint. A healthy NA has honest, responsive sheet metal.

## Engine and gearbox

The 1.6 B6 and 1.8 BP are hardy engines, but they have decades of use behind
them. Service history matters far more than the odometer reading.

1. Start cold and listen for the timing chain — no rattles allowed.
2. Watch for blue smoke when throttling after idle.
3. Confirm the gearbox slots cleanly into second and fifth.

If the owner has no paperwork for the BP's timing belt, assume you'll have
to replace it yourself.

## Verdict

An honest MX-5 NA shows itself in the details: dry boot, clean oil, a clutch
that doesn't slip and documented servicing. If any of those are missing,
negotiate hard or keep looking — there are plenty of NAs out there and
patience pays off.
